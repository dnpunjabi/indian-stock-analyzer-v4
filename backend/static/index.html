📚 Solvency & Quality Glossary ▼
Ratio A (Working Capital / Total Assets): Measures short-term liquidity. High working capital indicates a strong cushion to meet short-term obligations.
Ratio B (Retained Earnings / Total Assets): Measures cumulative profitability. High ratio shows strong historical earnings retention.
Ratio C (EBIT / Total Assets): Measures operating asset productivity. Shows how much earnings are generated per unit of assets.
Ratio D (Market Cap / Total Liabilities): Measures solvency leverage. A high ratio indicates strong equity cushion relative to total debt.
Ratio E (Sales / Total Assets): Measures asset turnover. Shows how efficiently management uses assets to generate revenue.
1. Net Income: Positive Net Profit after tax in the latest year.
2. ROA (Return on Assets): Positive Return on Assets in the latest year.
3. Cash Flow from Operations (CFO): Operating Cash Flow is positive.
4. Accrual Quality (CFO > PAT): Operating Cash Flow exceeds Net Profit (indicates high cash quality).
5. Decreased Leverage: Long-term debt to total assets ratio decreased YoY.
6. Increased Liquidity: Current ratio increased YoY.
7. No Share Dilution: Total shares outstanding did not increase during the year.
8. Gross Margin Expansion: Operating profit margin increased YoY.
9. Asset Turnover Expansion: Sales to total assets ratio increased YoY (operating efficiency).
